Asking for customer feedback is a crucial part of improving your business, but it’s important to do it right. According to a study by Qualtrics, 77% of consumers have a more favorable view of brands that ask for and accept customer feedback. To ensure that you’re asking for feedback in a way that benefits both your customers and your business, here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind:

## The Dos:

1\. Be specific: When asking for feedback, be clear about what you want to know. Whether it’s about a recent purchase, a specific interaction, or a particular aspect of your product or service, specificity helps customers provide more useful feedback.

2\. Make it easy: Use simple, user-friendly survey tools like those offered by Feedbackpro.io to make it as easy as possible for customers to share their thoughts. The easier it is, the more likely they are to participate.

3\. Express gratitude: Always thank customers for their feedback, regardless of whether it’s positive or negative. This shows that you value their input and are committed to using it to improve.

## The Don’ts:

1\. Don’t ask too often: Bombarding customers with feedback requests can be off-putting. Be strategic about when and how often you ask for feedback to avoid overwhelming your audience.

2\. Don’t make it a one-way street: Let customers know how their feedback has been used and what changes have been made as a result. This encourages ongoing participation and shows that their input is genuinely valued.

3\. Don’t ignore negative feedback: Negative feedback is an opportunity to learn and grow. Embrace it, and use it to make meaningful improvements to your business.
